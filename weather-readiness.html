Weather Readiness

Use these collapsible steps to prepare before watches and warnings turn into urgent decisions.

  • Check the forecast daily when storms, fire weather, or winter hazards are possible.
  • Use more than one alert source so you are not dependent on a single app or signal.
  • Understand the difference between a watch and a warning before conditions worsen.
  • Make sure overnight alerts can wake you if tornadoes or severe storms hit after dark.
  • Charge devices and backup batteries before storms arrive.
  • Store enough water if your home depends on a well pump or electric water system.
  • Use coolers, thermometers, and a simple fridge and freezer plan for outages.
  • Keep easy meals, manual can openers, and lighting ready for long evenings without power.
  • Secure outdoor furniture, tools, and lightweight equipment before high winds arrive.
  • Trim known problem branches and keep driveways clear for emergency access.
  • Check drainage paths, culverts, and low spots before heavy rain starts.
  • Move vehicles, trailers, and equipment out of flood-prone or tree-risk areas when possible.
  • Confirm shelter, fencing, and water access for pets and livestock before weather changes.
  • Keep feed, medications, and halters where they can be reached in the dark.
  • Plan for ventilation and water backup if outages affect barns or automatic systems.
  • Decide ahead of time whether animals shelter in place or move to a safer pasture or structure.
  • Fuel up earlier than usual when severe weather is likely to close roads or delay power restoration.
  • Tell someone your expected route if you may travel through isolated areas.
  • Keep paper maps, printed numbers, and a weather radio available when coverage is unreliable.
  • Watch for long driveway washouts, blocked culverts, and fallen lines after the storm passes.
