Local Resources and Contacts

Gather the numbers that save time when rural help is farther away or communication is limited.

  • List sheriff, fire, EMS, and 911 notes specific to your address or access route.
  • Write down cross streets, gate instructions, and landmarks rural responders may need.
  • Keep non-emergency numbers too, especially where dispatch systems vary by county.
  • Collect power, gas, water, propane, internet, and rural electric co-op numbers.
  • Note account numbers and map numbers so outage reporting goes faster.
  • Keep instructions for generator use and well-water backup near those contacts.
  • List nearby clinics, hospitals, urgent care, pharmacies, and Poison Control.
  • Include after-hours options if the nearest facility is far away.
  • Keep directions for the fastest route in bad weather or nighttime travel.
  • Keep small-animal and large-animal vets, after-hours clinics, and transport help numbers.
  • Write down barn directions, animal IDs, and emergency handling notes.
  • Include neighbors or helpers who can load or handle livestock in a hurry.
  • List county emergency management, local alert systems, and shelter information.
  • Keep your preferred forecast sources and local weather radio details together.
  • Note where to find local burn bans, evacuation notices, or shelter updates.
  • Keep state, county, and local road condition resources in one place.
  • Write down alternate routes if one bridge, culvert, or low-water crossing is a common failure point.
  • Include towing, tire, or heavy-equipment contacts that can help on remote roads.

These official and widely used U.S. emergency resources can help with outages, forecasts, family planning, wildfire activity, road conditions, and livestock preparedness.

  • PowerOutage.us - real-time outage map
  • FCC Emergency Communications Tips
  • NOAA Weather.gov - forecasts, warnings, and radar
  • NOAA Storm Prediction Center - severe storm outlooks
  • Ready.gov - family emergency planning
  • FEMA Preparedness Guides - printable checklists and planning resources
  • AirNow.gov - smoke and air quality
  • National Interagency Fire Center - wildfire activity
  • 511 Traveler Information Directory - road conditions by state
  • USDA Animal Emergency Management - livestock emergencies
